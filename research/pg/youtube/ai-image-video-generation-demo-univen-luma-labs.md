## Language Mix Pattern

Roughly 65-70% English tokens by content, but nearly every sentence is grammatically anchored in Hindi — this is true intra-sentence code-switching, not alternating between English and Hindi blocks.
- **Code-switching style**: He switches mid-sentence, often mid-clause, not at sentence boundaries. Examples:
  - `[00:19]` "So, in this particular video, I want to show you a model known as Univen on Luma labs." (English sentence with Hindi so/comma pacing)

  - `[01:20]` "Yahan par aap kya kar sakte ho? Aap multiple art boards bana sakte ho." — Hindi question, English noun ("art boards") dropped into a Hindi sentence.

- **Hindi filler/connector words used constantly**: so (so — used almost like a verbal comma at the start of nearly every beat), toh (then/so), okay? (okay? — checking in with viewer), theek hai? (right?/okay?), yahan par (here/at this point — used to anchor to the screen), matlab (I mean), ah (uh, hesitation filler), basically (basically — Hindi-pronunciation of English word, used like a native Hindi filler).

- **Enthusiastic but measured** — not shouty hype, more like an engaged practitioner showing off something he's genuinely impressed by. ## Teaching / Explaining Approach (Demo-Driven)

- **Show, don't just tell**: nearly the entire video is a live screen demo narrated step-by-step (sign-in → create board → write prompt → generate → inspect output → follow-up prompt → repeat with new use case).
- **States the "what" and "why" before the "how"**: opens by explaining the core differentiator (reasoning before generation) before touching the UI at all.
- **Uses concrete, specific prompts as teaching examples** rather than abstract descriptions, and reads them out loud to the viewer, e.g. `[03:53]` "Cinematic still of a deserted gas station at night with a A24 film style dramatic lighting 35mm ka a film grain."
- **Explains the underlying mechanism in simple terms** at the moment of action, not just the result: `[02:20]` "What it does is? Ye pehle reason karta hai. Ye pehle usko understand karta hai ki user chahta kya hai. So it does a reasoning, it does all the calculations, it does all the behind the things."
- **Escalates complexity gradually**: starts with a simple single prompt, then shows a "follow-up prompt" to refine the same generation (`[02:53]` adding a 3D style follow-up), demonstrating iterative/conversational use of the tool rather than one-shot only.
- **Compresses dead time explicitly for the viewer** ("thoda sa hum fast forward karte hain") instead of silently cutting, which doubles as a teaching signal ("generation takes a little while, that's normal").
- **Closes the loop with practical takeaways and resource pointers**: promises links in the description, and explicitly names the target audience who benefits ("agar aap ek developer hain, aap ek builder hain") before signing off — a consistent teach → demo → generalize → call-to-action structure.
